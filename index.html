Instructions

Required Materials

Gather everything below before you begin so you can move through each step without interruption.

A · Cosmetic Products

Skin TintLightweight, sheer base
Eyebrow GelTinted or clear formula for brow placement
ConcealerHigh-coverage cream or liquid for targeted areas
BronzerPowder or cream for warmth and contouring
BlushPink or peach-toned product for the cheeks
Setting PowderTranslucent or tinted powder to reduce shine
HighlighterShimmering powder or liquid for luminosity
Brown EyelinerPencil or gel liner for soft eye definition
LiplinerPencil used to define the lip perimeter
MascaraLengthening or volumizing formula for lashes

B · Application Tools

Damp Makeup SpongeFor blending base products
Large Fluffy BrushFor bronzer application
Tapered Blush BrushFor precise cheek color placement
Medium Powder BrushFor dusting setting powder
Small Fan or Precision BrushFor applying highlighter
Small Dense BrushFor blending concealer
Before you begin: Set up in a well-lit area with a mirror. Keep all materials within reach and complete each step in order — later steps build on earlier ones.
01

Apply Skin Tint

Dispense a small amount of skin tint and use your fingers or a damp makeup sponge to dot it onto the center of the face, blending outward in light, tapping motions until the finish is seamless and the skin tone is even.

02

Apply Eyebrow Gel

Define and set the eyebrows by using the built-in spoolie wand to comb the hairs in the direction they grow, then apply the gel in short, upward strokes following the natural arch to add definition.

03

Apply Concealer

Use the precision applicator to apply concealer in a "V" shape under the eyes and onto any blemishes, then gently blend the edges with your ring finger or a small, dense concealer brush so it merges perfectly with the base.

04

Apply Bronzer

Using a large, fluffy bronzer brush, lightly sweep the product along the temples, below the cheekbones, and across the hairline, blending thoroughly upward and outward to add warmth and dimension.

05

Apply Blush

Identify the "apples" of your cheeks by smiling slightly, then use a tapered blush brush to swirl the product onto the center and blend softly back toward the temples for a natural flush.

06

Apply Setting Powder

Lightly dip a medium, fluffy powder brush into the powder, tap off the excess, and gently dust the T-zone — forehead, nose, and chin — to lock the makeup in place and control shine.

07

Apply Highlighter

Use a small fan brush or your fingertip to apply highlighter along the high points of the face — the tops of the cheekbones, the bridge of the nose, and the Cupid's bow — blending well for a luminous finish.

08

Apply Brown Eyeliner

Gently pull the upper eyelid taut and use the eyeliner pencil or a fine-angled brush to apply the liner as close to the upper lash line as possible, starting from the inner corner and moving outward.

09

Apply Lipliner

Using the sharpened lipliner pencil, trace the natural perimeter of your lips starting at the center of the bottom lip and moving to the Cupid's bow to define the shape and prevent color bleeding.

10

Apply Mascara

Wiggle the mascara wand at the base of the upper lashes to deposit pigment, then comb upward through the lengths to lengthen and define the lashes for a finished look.

Purpose

The purpose of this Memoranda of Understanding (MoU) is to codify instructions as it pertains to makeup application between G3 and interested audience members within WRT 307 Cohort with the understanding that this exchange of knowledge is attainable as well as versatile concerning price point and shades of pigmentation. By declaring this agreement, both stakeholders seek to share feedback with one another through the promise of adaptable, expressive application. This MoU does not create a legally binding contract but serves as a declaration concerning continued interdependent skill-building and awareness in good faith and engagement.

Scope

The scope of this Memoranda of Understanding (MoU) is as follows: the instructions for our project, "A Beginner's Guide to Makeup Application," caters to interested audience members within WRT 307 Cohort who are applying makeup for the first time (or is interested in sharing an easy-to-follow tutorial with a friend). Commencing with required materials, we have sectioned off the listing of cosmetic products and application tools. Following this, we have sequentially arranged the application process for the cosmetic products, to which coincides with the recommended usage approach of the application tools. Through these instructions, we were able to assess the levels of success that our project volunteers experienced as well as pain points that they encountered.

The project was composed collaboratively with Ella hosting our HTML website, Kriston sourcing our project volunteers, Emma devising the instructions, Amirah proofreading our contributions, and Jamyka composing the professional memo.

Usability Report

Anticipated Problem Areas (Pre-Testing)

Before conducting user tests, our team identified the following areas where users were most likely to encounter difficulty:

  • Step 3 (concealer blending) — users may apply too much product or fail to fully blend edges.
  • Step 4 (bronzer placement) — the sweep direction and placement are not intuitive for beginners.
  • Step 8 (eyeliner) — staying close to the lash line requires practice and steady hand control.

Troubleshooting Guide

If your result does not match the instructions at any step, consult the troubleshooting tips below:

  • Skin tint looks patchy: The formula may have been applied to dry skin. Dampen your sponge more thoroughly and blend in smaller sections.
  • Bronzer looks muddy: You may have applied too much. Buff in circular motions with a clean brush to diffuse the product.
  • Eyeliner smudges: Allow the liner to set for 30 seconds before blinking; consider a waterproof formula.
